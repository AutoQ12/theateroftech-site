UK Advances Project Brakestop With £45M for Three Long-Range Strike Drones for Ukraine

The UK MoD has handed MBDA UK, MGI Engineering, and Rotron Aerospace roughly £45 million to push their one-way attack drone prototypes toward live trials in Ukraine under Project Brakestop.

SECTOR · UNMANNED VEHICLES PUBLISHED · 2026-06-25

UK Commits £45 Million to Accelerate Long-Range Strike Weapons for Ukraine

The British Ministry of Defence has put approximately £45 million into the next stage of Project Brakestop, its accelerated push to field affordable ground-launched strike weapons for Ukraine. The money goes to three competing British firms — MBDA UK, MGI Engineering, and Rotron Aerospace — each of which has already put a functioning prototype through its paces at the MoD's Hebrides Range in Scotland.

Each company takes home roughly £15 million under its follow-on contract, covering continued system development, 15 weapons apiece, and the launch and ground-support equipment to go with them, according to Army Technology.

From 27 Contenders to Three Finalists

Brakestop launched in late 2024 under the MoD's Taskforce Kindred, drawing proposals from 27 UK industrial teams. Six received early-phase contracts of around £5 million each to design and build testable prototypes within seven months. By December 2025 the field was down to three, all of whom completed flight-trial events at the Hebrides Range before the latest funding was announced.

The programme's performance bar is steep: the winning weapon must deliver a 225-kilogram warhead to targets at least 500 kilometres away while sustaining speeds above 600 kilometres per hour. The MoD says warhead development and testing are already finished, separate from the airframe competition.

MBDA's Crossbow Leads on Transparency

Of the three finalists, MBDA has been the most open about what it's offering. Its Crossbow weapon — a turbojet-powered design with a claimed range exceeding 800 kilometres — completed its first truck-launched firing in December 2025, then ran a second test campaign in February, according to FlightGlobal. MBDA says it is "able to produce Crossbow in 2026, at scale, at pace," a pitch that positions it as the most production-ready of the three.

Path to Ukraine Includes Live Overseas Trials

Domestic UK testing isn't the end of the road. The MoD has confirmed that further evaluations will take place overseas, with in-country trials in Ukraine explicitly written into the programme roadmap. Whoever wins the full production contract must prove they can manufacture at least 20 weapons per month within months of receiving the order — a requirement that reflects urgent operational need rather than a standard procurement schedule.

Why It Matters

Project Brakestop is one of the most concrete Western efforts to industrialise low-cost, long-range strike capability specifically for the Ukrainian conflict. With Russia's missile and drone campaigns continuing to hit Ukrainian infrastructure, Kyiv's ability to strike deep behind the front line with affordable, mass-producible weapons is a stated strategic priority. The programme also signals a broader shift in British defence procurement toward speed and volume over traditional cost-plus development cycles — a model that allies and adversaries alike will be watching closely.

UK Advances Project Brakestop With £45M for Three Long-Range Strike Drones for Ukraine

The UK MoD has awarded three companies roughly £45 million to advance development of low-cost, 500km-range one-way attack weapons under Project Brakestop, with live trials in Ukraine planned.

SECTOR · UNMANNED VEHICLES PUBLISHED · 2026-06-25

UK Commits £45 Million to Accelerate Long-Range Strike Weapons for Ukraine

The British Ministry of Defence has injected approximately £45 million into the next stage of Project Brakestop, its accelerated programme to field affordable ground-launched strike weapons for Ukraine. The funding is split across three competing British defence firms — MBDA UK, MGI Engineering, and Rotron Aerospace — each of which has now demonstrated a functioning prototype at the MoD's Hebrides Range in Scotland.

Each company receives roughly £15 million under its follow-on contract, covering continued system development, the supply of 15 weapons apiece, and associated launch and ground-support equipment, according to Army Technology.

From 27 Contenders to Three Finalists

Brakestop was launched in late 2024 under the MoD's Taskforce Kindred, initially attracting proposals from 27 UK industrial teams. Six of those were awarded early-phase contracts of around £5 million each to design and build testable prototypes within a seven-month window. By December 2025, the field had narrowed to the current three, all of whom completed flight-trial events at the Hebrides Range ahead of the latest funding announcement.

The programme's performance threshold is demanding: the selected weapon must carry a 225-kilogram warhead to targets at least 500 kilometres away and sustain speeds above 600 kilometres per hour. The MoD has confirmed that warhead development and testing are already complete independently of the airframe competition.

MBDA's Crossbow Leads on Transparency

Of the three finalists, MBDA has been the most publicly forthcoming about its candidate. The company's Crossbow weapon — a turbojet-powered design with a claimed range exceeding 800 kilometres — completed its first truck-launched firing in December 2025, followed by a second test campaign in February, according to FlightGlobal. MBDA says it is "able to produce Crossbow in 2026, at scale, at pace," positioning itself as the most production-ready of the three bidders.

Path to Ukraine Includes Live Overseas Trials

Beyond domestic UK testing, the MoD has confirmed that further evaluations will take place overseas, with in-country trials in Ukraine explicitly included in the programme roadmap. Whichever contractor ultimately wins the full production contract must demonstrate the ability to manufacture at least 20 weapons per month within months of receiving a production order — a requirement that reflects urgent operational demand rather than a conventional procurement timeline.

Why It Matters

Project Brakestop represents one of the most concrete Western efforts to industrialise low-cost, long-range strike capability specifically for the Ukrainian conflict. With Russia's missile and drone campaigns continuing to target Ukrainian infrastructure, Kyiv's ability to strike deep behind the front line with affordable, mass-producible weapons is a stated strategic priority. The programme also signals a broader shift in British defence procurement toward speed and volume over traditional cost-plus development cycles — a model that allies and adversaries alike will be watching closely.

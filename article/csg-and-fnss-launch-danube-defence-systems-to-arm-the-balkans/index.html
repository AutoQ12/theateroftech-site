CSG and FNSS Launch Danube Defence Systems to Arm the Balkans

A new joint venture between Croatian firm CSG and Turkish manufacturer FNSS will produce armoured vehicles — including a medium tank — in Europe, targeting Balkan and regional defence markets.

SECTOR · EUROPE PUBLISHED · 2026-06-23 HUMAN-VERIFIED

CSG and FNSS Establish Danube Defence Systems at Eurosatory 2026

A Croatian and Turkish defence partnership unveiled at the Eurosatory 2026 exhibition in Paris has created a new armoured vehicle producer aimed squarely at European and Balkan procurement needs, according to reporting by Janes.

Consolidated Savings Group (CSG) of Croatia and Turkish armoured vehicle manufacturer FNSS have formalised a joint venture called Danube Defence Systems, announced at the Paris defence show held 15–19 June. The partnership is designed to give European customers — particularly those across the Balkans — access to locally produced armoured platforms without relying entirely on transatlantic supply chains.

The centrepiece of the new venture's offering is the Karpat medium tank, a platform developed under the collaboration that will be manufactured in Europe. Beyond the tank, Danube Defence Systems intends to offer a full family of Karpat-variant vehicles, including armoured personnel carriers and infantry fighting vehicles. The portfolio will also encompass FNSS's broader range of wheeled and tracked vehicles along with the company's weapon system offerings — giving potential buyers a one-stop-shop for armoured capabilities.

The Balkans has emerged as one of the most active defence procurement regions in Europe, with multiple nations accelerating modernisation programmes under pressure from NATO commitments and the security lessons drawn from Russia's war in Ukraine. Croatia itself has been upgrading its ground forces, while neighbouring Serbia, Slovenia, and North Macedonia face varying timelines for platform replacement across their armoured fleets.

FNSS is an established player in the tracked and wheeled vehicle space, with exports across the Middle East, Southeast Asia, and parts of Europe. Partnering with a Croatian firm provides the Turkish manufacturer with a direct foothold inside the European Union's defence industrial base — a commercially and politically significant move at a time when EU procurement frameworks increasingly favour European-origin production.

The timing of the announcement is notable. The European Union's Security Action for Europe (SAFE) financing mechanism is emerging as a key funding vehicle for member-state defence purchases, and several Balkan EU members and candidates are actively exploring how to access it. A joint venture with European manufacturing credentials could position Danube Defence Systems to qualify for or benefit from such programmes.

No contracts or letters of intent with specific buyers were disclosed at Eurosatory, and the precise manufacturing location within Europe has not been publicly confirmed, leaving important commercial details unresolved.

Why it matters: The establishment of Danube Defence Systems reflects a broader trend of non-EU defence firms seeking European production partnerships to access a rapidly expanding market. For Balkan states facing urgent armour modernisation timelines, a regionally anchored supplier offering a medium tank family and associated platforms could prove a credible alternative to longer lead-time programmes from larger Western European primes.

Poland Acquires Shield AI V-BAT Drones for Baltic Sea Naval Operations

Poland has contracted California-based Shield AI to supply V-BAT drones to its navy for Baltic Sea operations, with Prime Minister Donald Tusk also revealing an offer for Poland to take a manufacturing role in Shield AI's autonomous X-BAT fighter jet programme.

SECTOR · EUROPE PUBLISHED · 2026-06-25

Poland Contracts Shield AI for Naval Drone Capability

The Polish Ministry of National Defence has signed a contract with California-based Shield AI to buy the company's V-BAT vertical take-off and landing drone for the Polish Navy, Defense News reports. Warsaw's goal is to extend its maritime surveillance and operational reach across the Baltic Sea, which has taken on heightened strategic importance since Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine.

The V-BAT needs no runway to launch or recover, making it well suited to shipborne operations in contested littoral environments. The deal puts Poland alongside a growing number of NATO allies putting money into autonomous naval unmanned systems.

X-BAT Manufacturing Offer Adds Industrial Dimension

The procurement comes with an offer that goes well beyond the hardware itself. Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk has revealed that Shield AI offered Poland a manufacturing role in its X-BAT programme, an autonomous vertical take-off fighter jet still in development. If the arrangement goes ahead, it would embed Poland in next-generation U.S. autonomous combat aviation, trading sovereign industrial capacity for access to cutting-edge technology.

A near-term operational purchase paired with a longer-term industrial partnership offer reflects a wider pattern across European defence, where procurement decisions are increasingly tied to domestic production stakes and technology transfer.

Baltic Security Drives Unmanned Investment

Poland has steadily expanded its defence budget and modernisation agenda in recent years, with Baltic Sea security a consistent priority. The V-BAT contract fits that framework, giving the Polish Navy an unmanned aerial surveillance asset that can operate from surface vessels without dedicated flight-deck infrastructure.

The timing also aligns with a broader surge in European defence investment. The FTSE 350 Aerospace & Defence Index climbed more than 85 percent between January 2025 and June 2026, according to Defense News, while venture funding into European defence startups reached €7.4 billion in 2025 alone. The EU's €150 billion SAFE loan programme is simultaneously pushing member states to scale up domestic and intra-European production.

Why It Matters

The V-BAT acquisition is a relatively contained step, but it shows that NATO's eastern flank isn't relying solely on legacy platforms to address evolving maritime threats. The potential X-BAT manufacturing role, if formalised, would represent a deeper industrial integration between Poland and a leading U.S. autonomous systems developer — precisely the kind of sovereign capability partnership that European defence planners have been pushing for as the continent accelerates its rearmament.
